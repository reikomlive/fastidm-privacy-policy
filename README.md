# Privacy Policy for fastidm

**Last updated: March 23, 2026**

## Overview

fastidm is a download manager for macOS. This privacy policy explains what data the app does and does not collect.

**Short version: fastidm collects no data about you. Ever.**

---

## Data We Collect

**None.**

fastidm does not collect, transmit, store, or share any personal information, usage data, analytics, crash reports, or any other data about you or your device.

---

## How the App Works

fastidm operates entirely on your local machine:

- **Downloads** go directly from the source server to your Mac. fastidm acts as a conduit — it never sees, stores, or logs the content of your downloads on any external server.
- **Download history** is stored locally in a SQLite database on your Mac. This data never leaves your device.
- **Settings** are stored locally using macOS standard preferences. They never leave your device.
- **Notifications** are local notifications only. When a download completes or fails, your Mac displays a notification. No notification server is involved.
- **Local server** — fastidm runs a local HTTP server on port 17099 exclusively for communication between the app and the browser extensions (Safari and Chrome) installed on the same machine. This server is not accessible from the internet and transmits no data outside your device.
- **Browser extensions** — the Safari and Chrome extensions communicate only with the local server on your Mac (127.0.0.1:17099). They do not transmit any data to external servers.
